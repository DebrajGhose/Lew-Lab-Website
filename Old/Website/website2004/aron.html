Aron Marguitz

My research focuses on understanding the various signaling pathways that contribute to the morphogenesis checkpoint. In the budding yeast Saccharomyces cerevisiae the morphogenesis checkpoint delays mitosis in response to environmental perturbations that delay bud formation. We have shown that cells lacking Bem2p do not delay mitosis when bud formation is blocked and are therefore defective in some aspect of the morphogenesis checkpoint. Bem2p is a 246 kD protein with a 160 amino acid Rho GAP domain at its carboxyl terminus, which has been reported to stimulate GTP hydrolysis by Rho1p. One response to checkpoint stimuli is the stabilization of the CDK-inhibitory kinase Swe1p, and surprisingly this occurs normally in bem2D cells. In addition perturbation of bud formation activates the MAP kinase Mpk1p, which appears to inhibit the CDK activating phosphatase Mih1p. We have shown that bem2 D cells activate Mpk1p to a similar extent compared to wild type cells, and deletion of MIH1 does not fully alleviate the bem2D checkpoint defect. This suggests that Bem2p has a role in a pathway leading to G2 arrest that has yet to be uncovered.

I am currently interested in defining a role for Bem2p in the checkpoint and uncovering the additional mechanisms for regulating the G2/M transition. Possible mechanisms by which Bem2p might affect the G2/M transition are by regulating the activity of the Swe1p kinase or Mih1p phosphatase (independent of Mpk1p). In order to determine if these processes are under regulation, I am developing in vitro and in vivo assays in which the activity of these enzymes can be measured independent from each other.
